The Challenge (And Why You Should Care)

Stigma

"পরিবারে এটি কী?" (What will the neighbors say?)

Mental health conditions are often misunderstood in rural communities, leading to social isolation and discrimination for those seeking help.

Access

The nearest psychiatric hospital is 80km away

Limited transportation options and long distances make it nearly impossible for rural residents to access specialized mental healthcare.

Awareness

Many don't know anxiety is a disease, not a personality flaw

Lack of education about mental health leads to misconceptions and prevents people from recognizing symptoms and seeking appropriate care.

Hablu's Reality Check

Hablu's Journey with Mental Health

A student from rural Bangladesh

My name is Hablu, and I'm a 21-year-old university student from a small village in Barisal Division. Like many students, I was focused on maintaining a good CGPA and making my family proud.

"I thought my constant worry and sleepless nights were just part of being a good student. I didn't realize I was struggling with anxiety until it became unbearable."

The pressure to perform well academically was immense. Every exam felt like a life-or-death situation. My heart would race, my hands would shake, and I couldn't focus on anything except the fear of failure. I thought this was normal - that everyone felt this way.

Things got worse when I started having panic attacks. I didn't know what they were. I thought I was dying. I was too ashamed to tell anyone. What would my parents say? What would the neighbors think?

It wasn't until a visiting doctor came to our community clinic that I learned about anxiety disorders. She explained that what I was experiencing was a medical condition, not a personal weakness. That day changed my life.

Today, I'm learning to manage my anxiety through simple techniques and regular check-ins. I'm also helping others in my village understand that mental health is just as important as physical health.
